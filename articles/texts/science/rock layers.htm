Rock Layers
If you were to take a chunk out of the earth's crust, you would notice some pretty visible layers in the rocks. These rock layers can tell a lot about our earth's history. Each rock layer contains different fossils from that era, period, and what not. A very important rule to understand when examining rock layers is that they always follow the rule of superposition. The rule of superposition states that the layer on top is always the youngest.
